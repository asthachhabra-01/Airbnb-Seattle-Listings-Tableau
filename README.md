# Airbnb Seattle Listings – Market Analysis Dashboard

A Tableau dashboard analysing Airbnb listing performance across Seattle, 
Washington - covering pricing by bedroom count, revenue trends, geographic 
distribution by zip code, and listing supply across neighbourhoods.

> **Disclaimer:** Dataset sourced from publicly available Airbnb listing data 
> (Inside Airbnb, January 2024). This project is built for portfolio and 
> learning purposes only and has no affiliation with Airbnb Inc.

---

## Objective

To analyse the Seattle short-term rental market and identify pricing patterns, 
revenue trends, and supply concentration across zip codes - providing a data 
foundation for host pricing strategy and market entry decisions.

---

- **Tableau Public** - dashboard design and interactive visualisation

## Key Insights

**1. Price scales sharply with bedroom count**
Average price increases consistently from $96 for 1-bedroom listings to $585 
for 6-bedroom properties - confirming that larger homes command a significant 
premium and represent higher revenue potential per booking.

**2. 1-bedroom listings dominate supply**
With over 1,800 distinct listings, 1-bedroom properties make up the vast 
majority of Seattle's Airbnb supply - creating a highly competitive segment 
where pricing and differentiation are critical for hosts.

**3. Zip code 98134 commands the highest average price**
At over $200 per night, 98134 (SoDo / industrial waterfront) leads all zip 
codes on average price - likely driven by a small number of large, high-value 
listings skewing the average upward.

**4. Revenue growth is strong through mid-year then plateaus**
The weekly revenue trend shows rapid growth from February through May 2024, 
followed by a plateau through the remainder of the year - suggesting seasonal 
demand peaks in spring and early summer, with relatively stable but flat 
performance from June onwards.

**5. Mid-range zip codes offer the most balanced supply and pricing**
Zip codes in the $125 to $175 average price range — including 98119 (Queen Anne) 
and 98109 - represent a strong balance of supply volume and price point, making 
them the most competitive and active segments of the Seattle market.
